\section{PDC fine tuning (2026-02-27)}

\begin{frame}{Goal and Fixed Conditions}
    \textbf{Optimization target:}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Keep magnetic field and target condition fixed at \textbf{B=1.15 T} and \textbf{target angle = 3 deg}.
        \item Tune only PDC geometry \textbf{(PDC1 X, PDC2 X, PDC angle)}.
        \item Validate proton acceptance for generated phase space:
        \item $\left(p_x,0,627\right)$ MeV/c with $p_x \in [-150,150]$ MeV/c.
    \end{itemize}
    \vspace{0.3cm}
    \textbf{Fixed geometry conditions during scan:}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Target position: $(-1.2488,\ 0.0009,\ -106.9458)$ cm
        \item PDC $z$ positions: $z_1=400$ cm, $z_2=500$ cm
    \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[fragile]{Scan Method and Metrics}
    \textbf{Two-stage Geant4 scan workflow}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Coarse scan: independent $x_1/x_2 \in [-60,60]$ cm (step 20 cm), angle $\in [60,70]$ deg (step 1 deg).
        \item Fine scan: around top coarse seeds with local offsets
        \item $x_{1,2}\pm 10$ cm (step 5 cm), angle $\pm 1$ deg (step 0.5 deg).
        \item Beam sampling: $p_x=-150:10:150$ MeV/c, $p_z=627$ MeV/c.
    \end{itemize}
    \vspace{0.2cm}
    \textbf{Ranking metrics (same as scan script):}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Primary: edge acceptance minimum at $|p_x|\ge 130$ MeV/c (\texttt{edge\_min}, larger is better).
        \item Secondary: acceptance uniformity CV over $p_x$ bins (\texttt{eff\_cv}, smaller is better).
        \item Tertiary: incidence-angle RMS (\texttt{incidence\_rms\_deg}, smaller is better).
        \item Additional monitor: total track efficiency (\texttt{track\_eff}).
    \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[fragile]{Production Run Record}
\begin{lstlisting}[language=bash]
python3 scripts/analysis/run_pdc_geant4_position_scan.py \

    \frametitle{Rationale for Target Placement}
    
    Why the target must be placed inside the magnetic field:
    
    \vspace{0.5cm}
    
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \textbf{Strong $B$-field Case:} 
        \begin{itemize}
            \item Difficult proton track reconstruction.
        \end{itemize}
        
        \item \textbf{Weak $B$-field Case:} 
        \begin{itemize}
            \item Risk of protons hitting the exit window.
        \end{itemize}
        
        \item \textbf{General Constraint:} 
        \begin{itemize}
            \item Poor neutron geometric acceptance.
        \end{itemize}
    \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\subsection{nebula acceptance}

\begin{frame}
    

The neutron acceptance depends on the beam bending angle. Although different magnetic field settings cause slight differences in position, the impact of the magnetic field is negligible. The angle should be smaller than $10^{\circ}$. Furthermore, the neutron distribution does not correlate with the proton changes; for neutrons within a specific region, the corresponding protons are distributed across nearly the entire space, indicating no significant correlation.

% 某一个区域内中子, 对应的质子也几乎弥散在全部的空间上 ,没有明显的相关性. (翻译此句话)

For neutrons within a specific region, the corresponding protons are distributed across nearly the entire space, indicating no significant correlation.
\end{frame}
