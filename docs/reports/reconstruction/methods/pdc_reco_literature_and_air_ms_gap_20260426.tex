\section{TL;DR}
This document surveys the published reference frame for the SAMURAI PDC1/PDC2 drift-chamber chain and contrasts it with our current Geant4 reconstruction performance, in order to localise the air multiple-scattering gap that drives our PDC2 spatial resolution one order of magnitude above design.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Reference frame.} The PDC1/PDC2 chain has no dedicated peer-reviewed publication. The canonical references are the SAMURAI overview paper by Kobayashi et al., Nucl. Instr. Meth. B \textbf{317}, 294--304 (2013) §3.4\footnote{\label{fn:kobayashi2013}T.~Kobayashi et al., Nucl. Instr. Meth. B \textbf{317}, 294--304 (2013), DOI: \href{https://doi.org/10.1016/j.nimb.2013.05.089}{10.1016/j.nimb.2013.05.089}.}, the RIKEN internal technical note \texttt{Detector-PDC.pdf}\footnote{\label{fn:detectorpdc}RIKEN Nishina Center, \emph{Detector-PDC} technical note, \url{https://www.nishina.riken.jp/ribf/SAMURAI/image/Detector-PDC.pdf}.}, and the Kahlbow PhD thesis (TU Darmstadt, 2019)\footnote{\label{fn:kahlbow2019}J.~Kahlbow, PhD thesis, TU Darmstadt (2019), TUprints 9192, \url{https://tuprints.ulb.tu-darmstadt.de/9192/}.} which documents an independent reconstruction of the same hardware.
  \item \textbf{Design specifications.} Combining Kobayashi 2013 §3.4\footref{fn:kobayashi2013} with the SAMURAI 2012 construction proposal\footnote{\label{fn:samurai2012}SAMURAI Collaboration, \emph{SAMURAI Construction Proposal} (2012), \url{https://ribf.riken.jp/SAMURAI/120425_SAMURAIConstProp.pdf}.}, the PDC1/PDC2 chain targets 1~mm position resolution, 2~mrad angular resolution, and 0.8\% relative momentum resolution ($\Delta p/p$). Commissioning RI-beam runs reached $\Delta p/p \approx 1/1500$\footref{fn:kobayashi2013}; the original proton-mode design value is $\Delta p/p \approx 1/700$ as quoted in the RIBF-TAC05 (2005) construction proposal\footnote{\label{fn:ribftac05}SAMURAI Collaboration, \emph{RIBF-TAC05 SAMURAI construction proposal} (2005), \url{https://ribf.riken.jp/RIBF-TAC05/10_SAMURAI.pdf}.}.
  \item \textbf{Our measured $\sigma_y$(PDC2).} In our Geant4 + reconstruction chain we observe $\sigma_y(\text{PDC2}) = 12.83$--$14.93$~mm in the all-air configuration, $6.80$--$8.21$~mm when Region~A (dipole cavity + beam pipe) is set to vacuum (\texttt{beamline\_vacuum}), and approximately $1.0$~mm in the all-vacuum configuration\footnote{\label{fn:msablation}See \texttt{docs/reports/reconstruction/momentum\_reco/ms\_ablation/ms\_ablation\_mixed\_20260422\_zh.md} §4.}. The realistic-air values sit roughly an order of magnitude away from the 1~mm hardware design specification\footref{fn:kobayashi2013}.
  \item \textbf{Air MS dominates and the configuration choice matters.} A quadrature decomposition\footref{fn:msablation} attributes the all-air budget to $\sigma_A \approx 11.19$~mm from Region~A (cavity + beam pipe, $\sim$4.3~m) and $\sigma_B \approx 7.43$~mm from Region~B (Exit Window $\to$ PDCs, $\sim$2~m). The stage~D inputs from the same memo\footnote{See \texttt{docs/reports/reconstruction/momentum\_reco/ms\_ablation/ms\_ablation\_mixed\_20260422\_zh.md} §6.} give $\sigma_y(\text{PDC2}) = 7.49$~mm if Region~A is vacuum and $\sigma_y(\text{PDC2}) = 13.50$~mm if both Region~A and Region~B are air. Whether Region~A is air or vacuum during the experiment is therefore a configuration question that selects between these two values; this document does not assert which one corresponds to the actual run.
\end{itemize}

See §6 for proposed action items.

\subsection{Geometry of the proton flight path}
The deuteron target sits at world coordinates $(-12.488, 0.0009, -1069.458)$~mm, which corresponds to yoke-local coordinates $(-545.5, 0, -919.9)$~mm under the $-30^\circ$ yoke rotation about $Y$\footnote{\label{fn:msablation_geom}See \texttt{docs/reports/reconstruction/momentum\_reco/ms\_ablation/ms\_ablation\_mixed\_20260422\_zh.md} §1.1--§1.3 for coordinates, yoke rotation, and Region A/B definitions.}. Because the SAMURAI dipole cavity (\texttt{vchamber\_box}) extends over $z \in [-1750, +1750]$~mm in yoke-local frame, the target at $z=-919.9$~mm is \emph{physically inside the dipole cavity} rather than upstream of it. Two regions therefore separate the target from the PDCs. \textbf{Region~A} ($\approx 4.3$~m) covers the in-cavity flight from the target through the cavity exit (2.67~m) and onwards through the downstream beam-pipe (\texttt{VacuumDownstream}, $\approx 1.6$~m) up to the Exit Window; this region is controlled by the Geant4 macro switch \texttt{/samurai/geometry/BeamLineVacuum}. \textbf{Region~B} ($\approx 2.0$~m) covers free flight in the world volume from the Exit Window at world $(170.5, 0, 3187.5)$~mm to PDC1 at $(700, 0, 4000)$~mm (0.97~m) and on to PDC2 (1.00~m), gated by \texttt{/samurai/geometry/FillAir}. Inside Region~A the proton bends $\sim 30^\circ$ in the 1.15~T field, but the arc-length vs.\ straight-line difference stays below 10\%\footref{fn:msablation_geom}.

\subsection{Why this matters for the science floor}
The PDC2 $\sigma_y$ entries above are not the end of the story: they propagate backwards through the Runge--Kutta tracker and the SAMURAI magnetic field map to a target-momentum uncertainty $\sigma_{p,\text{target}}$, which is the reconstruction science floor for any experiment in which the target sits inside the magnet and the outgoing proton is measured downstream (target $\to$ magnet $\to$ PDC). This document does not re-derive $\sigma_{p,\text{target}}$ propagation values; the per-method numerical breakdown lives in the RK error-analysis deep-dive memo\footnote{\label{fn:rkdeepdive}See \texttt{docs/reports/reconstruction/momentum\_reco/rk/rk\_error\_analysis\_deep\_dive\_20260426\_zh.pdf} for $\sigma_{p,\text{target}}$ propagation under MC, Profile, Laplace, MCMC, and MC-PDC.}.

\subsection{Hardware foundations}
The PDC1/PDC2 hardware specification is documented across four canonical sources: Kobayashi 2013 §3.4\footref{fn:kobayashi2013}, the RIKEN internal \texttt{Detector-PDC.pdf} tech note\footref{fn:detectorpdc}, the SAMURAI 2012 construction proposal\footref{fn:samurai2012}, and the older RIBF-TAC05 (2005) construction proposal\footref{fn:ribftac05}. The proton-arm magnet is the SAMURAI superconducting H-shape dipole described by Sato et al., providing a 7~Tm bending power, a maximum field of 3~T, an 80~cm pole gap and a 2~m pole face, with the field map computed in OPERA-3d / TOSCA\footnote{\label{fn:sato2013}H.~Sato et al., IEEE Trans. Appl. Supercond. \textbf{23}, 4500308 (2013), DOI: \href{https://doi.org/10.1109/TASC.2012.2237225}{10.1109/TASC.2012.2237225}.}. The drift cells follow the Walenta geometry with an 8~mm drift distance, arranged as five planes (U/V/X/U/V) at $\pm 45^\circ/0^\circ$ over a $1700 \times 800$~mm$^2$ active area, filled with Ar + 25\% iC$_4$H$_{10}$ (or alternatively Ar + 50\% C$_2$H$_6$) and read out via $\sim$810 channels\footref{fn:detectorpdc}. The chambers sit at $\sim 59^\circ$ on the proton arm with the design targets of 1~mm position resolution, 2~mrad angular resolution, and 0.8\% relative momentum resolution, with commissioning RI runs reaching $\Delta p/p \approx 1/1500$\footref{fn:kobayashi2013} and a proton-mode design specification of $\Delta p/p \approx 1/700$\footref{fn:ribftac05}. The signal chain feeds an ASD shaper into AMT-VME TDCs at 0.78~ns/ch\footref{fn:ribftac05}, where the AMT chip is the TDC ASIC originally described by Arai\footnote{\label{fn:arai2000}Y.~Arai, Nucl. Instr. Meth. A \textbf{453}, 365 (2000), DOI: \href{https://doi.org/10.1016/S0168-9002(00)00658-6}{10.1016/S0168-9002(00)00658-6}.}. The boundary between the dipole-cavity vacuum (Region~A) and the downstream air volume (Region~B) is the SAMURAI Exit Window described by Shimizu et al.: a Kevlar foil of 280~µm backed by a Mylar foil of 75~µm\footnote{\label{fn:shimizu2013}Y.~Shimizu et al., Nucl. Instr. Meth. B \textbf{317}, 739--742 (2013), DOI: \href{https://doi.org/10.1016/j.nimb.2013.08.059}{10.1016/j.nimb.2013.08.059}.}. The matching offline reconstruction pipeline is the RIKEN ROOT-based drift-chamber analysis described by Otsu et al.\footnote{\label{fn:otsu2016}H.~Otsu et al., Nucl. Instr. Meth. B \textbf{376}, 175 (2016), DOI: \href{https://doi.org/10.1016/j.nimb.2016.02.056}{10.1016/j.nimb.2016.02.056}.}. Together these four canonical sources cover the hardware geometry and design specifications, but none of them publishes a head-to-head proton-arm reconstruction performance paper; that gap is the motivation for §3.2 (algorithm spine) and §3.3 (performance achieved).

\subsection{Algorithm spine}
The published reference reconstruction chain for the SAMURAI proton arm follows a four-stage spine. \textbf{(i)~Hits to track segments.} Drift times measured by the AMT-VME TDCs are converted to drift distances and combined into a straight-line track in each PDC plane; this drift-time-to-distance and per-plane line-fit pipeline is the RIKEN ROOT-based drift-chamber analysis described by Otsu et al.\footref{fn:otsu2016}, deployed within the SAMURAIANA / \texttt{anaroot} framework (cite by repo path \texttt{anaroot/}). \textbf{(ii)~Back-tracking through the magnet.} The PDC-plane track is propagated backwards through the SAMURAI superconducting H-dipole using a Runge--Kutta integrator over the OPERA-3d / TOSCA field map published by Sato et al.\footref{fn:sato2013}; the field map itself is peer-reviewed, but the back-tracking algorithmic recipe is documented only in PhD theses (see point~iii) and not in any peer-reviewed paper. \textbf{(iii)~Optional Kalman smoothing.} A Kalman-filter smoother on the PDC tracks is the standard refinement, but \emph{PDC-side Kalman smoothing has no peer-reviewed publication; the full methodology lives only in PhD theses.} The closest published paper, Santamaria et al., Nucl. Instr. Meth. A \textbf{905}, 138--148 (2018)\footnote{\label{fn:santamaria2018}C.~Santamaria et al., Nucl. Instr. Meth. A \textbf{905}, 138--148 (2018), DOI: \href{https://doi.org/10.1016/j.nima.2018.07.053}{10.1016/j.nima.2018.07.053}.}, applies a Kalman filter to the MINOS TPC, not the PDCs. The PDC-side methodology is recorded across four PhD theses: Santamaria 2015 (Univ.\ Paris-Saclay)\footnote{\label{fn:santamaria2015phd}C.~Santamaria, PhD thesis, Univ.\ Paris-Saclay (2015), HAL tel-01231191, \url{https://hal.science/tel-01231191}.}, Kahlbow 2019 (TU Darmstadt)\footref{fn:kahlbow2019}, Lehr ca.\ 2023 (TU Darmstadt)\footnote{\label{fn:lehr2023phd}C.~Lehr, PhD thesis, TU Darmstadt (2023).}, and Storck 2023 (TU Darmstadt)\footnote{\label{fn:storck2023phd}S.~Storck, PhD thesis, TU Darmstadt (2023), TUprints 23768, \url{https://tuprints.ulb.tu-darmstadt.de/23768/}.}. \textbf{(iv)~Output.} The propagated state at the target plane yields the target-frame momentum vector $\vec p_{\text{target}}$ used downstream. As context, several MINOS+SAMURAI groups generate their training and validation data using the NPTool simulation framework of Matta et al., J.\ Phys.\ G \textbf{43}, 045113 (2016)\footnote{\label{fn:matta2016}A.~Matta et al., J.\ Phys.\ G \textbf{43}, 045113 (2016), DOI: \href{https://doi.org/10.1088/0954-3899/43/4/045113}{10.1088/0954-3899/43/4/045113}.}, but NPTool is a simulation framework, not the reconstruction path itself.

Our internal pipeline lives in \texttt{libs/analysis\_pdc\_reco/} (see also \texttt{smsimulator5.5/CLAUDE.md} for the repo-level guide) and follows the same Runge--Kutta back-tracking spine, with multiple selectable RK fit modes; one concrete example is \texttt{-{}-rk-fit-mode fixed-target-pdc-only} as exercised in the MS ablation memo\footref{fn:msablation} §3. An alternative neural-network backend is provided under \texttt{scripts/reconstruction/nn\_target\_momentum/}, and an RK geometry filter (\texttt{libs/qmd\_geo\_filter/}, with the matching skill \texttt{skills/smsim-rk-geometry-filter/}) prunes obviously off-acceptance trajectories before the fit. We do not re-derive the per-method numerical performance here: the full status, including MC, Profile, Laplace, MCMC, and MC-PDC variants, is reported in the RK status memo at \texttt{docs/reports/reconstruction/momentum\_reco/rk/rk\_reconstruction\_status\_20260416\_zh.pdf} and the deep-dive\footref{fn:rkdeepdive}. A detailed algorithm-by-algorithm comparison between the literature spine and our internal pipeline is deferred to §4.2 (Methodology gap).

\section{Gap analysis}
% [content from Tasks 7--8]
\subsection{Performance: \texorpdfstring{$\sigma$}{sigma} values}
The published reference frame sets the bar at the Kobayashi 2013 §3.4 design specification: 1~mm position resolution, 2~mrad angular resolution, and 0.8\% relative momentum resolution at the PDC, with the SAMURAI commissioning RI-beam runs reaching $\Delta p/p \approx 1/1500$\footref{fn:kobayashi2013}. The most recent public physics-grade comparison point is the SEASTAR-III $^{28}$O measurement of Kondo et al.\footnote{Y.~Kondo et al., Nature \textbf{620}, 965--970 (2023), DOI: \href{https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-023-06352-6}{10.1038/s41586-023-06352-6}.}, which quotes a 0.2~MeV FWHM decay-energy resolution; propagating that to a PDC-side relative momentum resolution gives $\sigma_p/p \sim 1\%$ at the order-of-magnitude level, but the figure is inferred (not directly reported in the paper) and is flagged as such here.

In our own Geant4 + reconstruction chain, the directly observable spatial spread is $\sigma_y(\text{PDC2}) = 12.83$--$14.93$~mm in the all-air configuration, $6.80$--$8.21$~mm when Region~A (dipole cavity + beam pipe) is set to vacuum (\texttt{beamline\_vacuum}), and approximately $1.0$~mm in the all-vacuum configuration; the corresponding $\sigma_{\theta_y}$ falls in 5.4--9.2~mrad for the all-air and \texttt{beamline\_vacuum} cases and 2.0--2.6~mrad for the all-vacuum case\footref{fn:msablation}. We do not introduce new numbers in this subsection; all entries are reproduced from Table~\ref{tab:sigma-three-conditions} in §2.

The position $\sigma_y$ at PDC2 is therefore an order of magnitude away from the 1~mm hardware design figure in both the all-air and the \texttt{beamline\_vacuum} configurations, and the angular $\sigma_{\theta_y}$ is 2--4$\times$ the 2~mrad design value in those same two configurations. Only the all-vacuum ablation reaches the design floor: $\sigma_y(\text{PDC2}) \approx 1.0$~mm matches the hardware specification to within the ablation precision, and $\sigma_{\theta_y} = 2.0$--$2.6$~mrad straddles the 2~mrad target. We do not yet have an end-to-end $\sigma_p/p$ figure from this ablation; the per-method $\sigma_{p,\text{target}}$ propagation methodology under MC, Profile, Laplace, MCMC, and MC-PDC is documented in the RK error-analysis deep-dive memo\footref{fn:rkdeepdive} and is not re-derived here.

The interpretation of this gap structure matters for how follow-up work is scoped. The fact that the all-vacuum ablation lands on the 1~mm hardware design floor is direct evidence that the reconstruction methodology itself is sound: the same RK back-tracking + drift-chamber pipeline that produces the 13~mm number in air produces the 1~mm number in vacuum, with no algorithmic change between the two. The gap to design is therefore primarily a material-budget problem rather than a methodology problem, and the responsibility for closing it sits with §4.3 (material budget and vacuum configuration). The $\sigma_y$(PDC2) entries propagate backwards through the RK tracker and the SAMURAI field map to a target-momentum uncertainty $\sigma_{p,\text{target}}$, and that propagation is captured method-by-method in the RK deep-dive\footref{fn:rkdeepdive}.

\subsection{Methodology}
The published methodology spine for the SAMURAI proton arm has three pieces. RK back-tracking through the Sato 2013 OPERA-3d / TOSCA field map\footref{fn:sato2013} forms the propagator; the front-end drift-chamber pipeline (drift-time-to-distance, per-plane line fit) is the RIKEN ROOT-based analysis of Otsu et al.\footref{fn:otsu2016}; an optional Kalman smoother on the PDC tracks is the standard refinement step but has \emph{no peer-reviewed paper covering the PDC-side methodology}. The closest published Kalman paper is Santamaria et al.\ Nucl. Instr. Meth. A \textbf{905} (2018)\footref{fn:santamaria2018}, which applies a Kalman filter to the MINOS TPC rather than the PDCs. The actual PDC-side Kalman methodology lives only in PhD theses: Santamaria 2015\footref{fn:santamaria2015phd}, Kahlbow 2019\footref{fn:kahlbow2019}, Lehr 2023\footref{fn:lehr2023phd}, and Storck 2023\footref{fn:storck2023phd}.

Our internal pipeline lives in \texttt{libs/analysis\_pdc\_reco/} (with the repo-level guide in \texttt{smsimulator5.5/CLAUDE.md}) and matches the literature spine piece-for-piece: it provides the same RK back-tracking propagator, exposes multiple selectable RK fit modes (one concrete example being \texttt{-{}-rk-fit-mode fixed-target-pdc-only} as exercised in the MS ablation memo\footref{fn:msablation}), wraps a neural-network backend under \texttt{scripts/reconstruction/nn\_target\_momentum/}, and runs a geometry filter (\texttt{libs/qmd\_geo\_filter/}) before the fit. The full numerical status of the RK pipeline, including MC, Profile, Laplace, MCMC, and MC-PDC variants, is recorded in the RK status memo\footnote{\label{fn:rkstatus}See \texttt{docs/reports/reconstruction/momentum\_reco/rk/rk\_reconstruction\_status\_20260416\_zh.pdf} for the per-method RK reconstruction status.} and the deep-dive\footref{fn:rkdeepdive}.

The methodological gap to the published reference frame is therefore small in algorithm shape: literature and our pipeline use the same RK back-tracking and the same optional Kalman smoothing. The \emph{actionable} gap is process-noise calibration. None of the four PhD theses or the Santamaria 2018 NIM A paper\footref{fn:santamaria2018} publishes a process-noise covariance tuned to a specific magnet + air configuration, and there is no public table that maps a given dipole + cavity + beam-pipe + downstream-air configuration to a Kalman process-noise covariance one can drop into our smoother. The quadrature decomposition in the MS ablation memo\footref{fn:msablation} §6 (with $\sigma_A \approx 11.19$~mm from Region~A and $\sigma_B \approx 7.43$~mm from Region~B) is exactly the per-region calibration data needed to set process-noise inputs for our specific geometry, and it does not exist in the public literature for this configuration.

The interpretation is that the published reference frame supplies the methodology shape (RK + optional Kalman), but it does not supply the configuration-specific tuning numbers, so we have to self-calibrate the process-noise inputs against our own ablation data rather than read them off a literature table. This is a closeable gap rather than a fundamental obstacle: the calibration data already exists internally\footref{fn:msablation}, and feeding it into the Kalman smoother is the next concrete methodology step.
\subsection{Material budget and vacuum configuration}
The published reference frame for the SAMURAI vacuum system is Shimizu et al.\ Nucl. Instr. Meth. B \textbf{317}, 739--742 (2013)\footref{fn:shimizu2013}, which documents the dipole cavity as evacuated and the boundary to the downstream air volume as the SAMURAI Exit Window (Kevlar 280~µm + Mylar 75~µm). Whether the SEASTAR-III / SAMURAI21 (2017) campaign in particular kept this evacuated-cavity configuration is \emph{inferred from Shimizu 2013 plus standard SAMURAI21 operation}; we do not have a direct quote in the surveyed references that explicitly confirms the Region~A vacuum status for SAMURAI21 (2017), and the claim is flagged as inferred rather than quoted.

In our own Geant4 setup, the Region~A vacuum status is under exploration rather than fixed. Two candidates from the MS ablation memo\footref{fn:msablation} §6 frame the choice numerically: with Region~A set to vacuum the stage~D PDC2 spread is $\sigma_y(\text{PDC2}) = 7.49$~mm, and with Region~A set to air it rises to $\sigma_y(\text{PDC2}) = 13.50$~mm. Both candidates are documented and neither is asserted to be the actual experimental run condition.

If the literature default is the evacuated cavity (inferred) and our $\sigma_y$ under that configuration is 7.49~mm, the residual gap to the 1~mm hardware design figure is dominated by Region~B (Exit Window $\to$ PDCs, $\approx 2$~m of air); if instead our actual configuration is an air-filled cavity giving 13.50~mm, the gap is dominated by Region~A. The quadrature decomposition in the same memo\footref{fn:msablation} §5.2 gives $\sigma_A \approx 11.19$~mm from Region~A and $\sigma_B \approx 7.43$~mm from Region~B, so Region~A air vs.\ vacuum is the single most consequential configuration question for closing the $\sigma$ gap.

Per the project's discipline on experimental-reality claims, we deliberately do not assert which of the two Region~A configurations corresponds to the actual experimental run; documenting both 7.49~mm and 13.50~mm candidates is the honest position. The actionable next step is collaborator confirmation of the SAMURAI21 (2017) Region~A status --- this is an engineering / operational question rather than a physics one, and it should be settled by reading the run-time vacuum logs or by direct contact with the SAMURAI operations group rather than by simulation alone.

\subsection{Experimental configuration and geometry}
The published reference frame for the SAMURAI + MINOS terminal places the reaction target outside the dipole magnet: the MINOS programme of Obertelli et al., EPJA \textbf{50}, 8 (2014)\footnote{\label{fn:obertelli2014}A.~Obertelli et al., Eur. Phys. J. A \textbf{50}, 8 (2014), DOI: \href{https://doi.org/10.1140/epja/i2014-14008-y}{10.1140/epja/i2014-14008-y}.} proposes a thick LH$_2$ target surrounded by a TPC vertex tracker upstream of the dipole, with a typical SEASTAR beam energy of $\sim 250$~MeV/u; the implementation paper of Santamaria et al.\ NIM~A \textbf{905}, 138--148 (2018)\footref{fn:santamaria2018} documents the MINOS TPC tracking and confirms the target-outside-magnet geometry.

In our setup, the deuteron target sits at world coordinates $(-12.488, 0.0009, -1069.458)$~mm and is therefore physically inside the SAMURAI dipole cavity rather than upstream of it\footref{fn:msablation_geom}. The proton kinematics is fixed at $|\vec p| = 627$~MeV/c, corresponding to $\sim 190$~MeV/u kinetic energy\footref{fn:msablation}; there is no vertex detector around the target.

Region~B ($\sim 2.0$~m, Exit Window $\to$ PDCs) is \emph{directly comparable} to the literature: same SAMURAI dipole, same Exit Window (Kevlar 280~µm + Mylar 75~µm), same PDC1/PDC2 chain, so any performance number on Region~B alone should benchmark against the Kahlbow 2019 PhD\footref{fn:kahlbow2019}. Region~A ($\sim 4.3$~m, target-inside-cavity flight) has \emph{no literature precedent}: published SAMURAI+MINOS configurations all place the target outside the magnet, so the in-cavity 4.3~m flight path is a configuration unique to our experiment.

The interpretation is that the geometry difference between literature and our experiment is by design --- our experiment puts the target inside the dipole cavity for physics reasons, while MINOS puts it outside for vertex-tracking reasons --- and is therefore not actionable in the sense that we should change the geometry to chase a literature number. What it does explain is why no published $\sigma_p/p$ figure applies to our setup directly: every public benchmark is for a target-outside-magnet configuration, and ours is the first target-inside-magnet case for this PDC chain. The Region~B comparison against Kahlbow 2019\footref{fn:kahlbow2019} remains the cleanest benchmark we can run, and Region~A has to be self-calibrated against our own ablation data.

The SEASTAR (``Shell Evolution And Search for Two-plus energies At the RIBF'') programme had two distinct hardware phases. \subsection{Open questions}
The following items are deliberately phrased as questions; none of them is closed by the current document.
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Is the actual SAMURAI21 (2017) Region~A air or vacuum?} This is under exploration.   \item \textbf{Is our PDC alignment consistent with the literature standard?} No PDC alignment data or alignment-comparison study has been produced internally to date; this is a follow-up question rather than a settled point, and a literature-vs-our-state alignment comparison has not been attempted in this document.
